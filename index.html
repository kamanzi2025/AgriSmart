🌍 Soil Management

🌱 Organic Matter

Apply 2–4 t/ha well-decomposed compost or farmyard manure before planting each season.
Incorporate green manure (e.g. Tithonia) 3–4 weeks before planting.
Target organic matter content: ≥ 3%

🧪 Fertiliser Application

Beans fix atmospheric nitrogen — avoid high-N fertilisers to protect nodulation.
Apply starter P fertiliser (TSP at 40 kg P₂O₅/ha) at planting.
Apply K fertiliser (MOP at 40 kg K₂O/ha) where soils are K-deficient.

🦠 Rhizobium Inoculation

Inoculate seed with Rhizobium phaseoli before planting — boosts N fixation by up to 80 kg N/ha/season.
Apply inoculant in shade; avoid direct sunlight and heat.

🔄 Recommended 3-Season Rotation

Season A → Beans (legume – adds nitrogen to soil)
Season B → Maize or Sorghum (cereal – uses the fixed N)
Season C → Sweet Potato or Cassava (root crop – breaks disease cycle)

✅ Rules

Never plant beans after beans on the same plot.
Cereals following beans need 30–40% less nitrogen fertiliser.
Leave at least one season before replanting beans on the same plot.

🌽 Intercropping Option

Climbing beans + Maize (2:1 row ratio) is widely practised in Rwanda and Kenya. Maize acts as a natural pole for climbing beans.

⚗ Optimal pH Range

Beans thrive at soil pH 5.5 – 6.5
Below pH 5.5: aluminium/manganese toxicity limits yield.
Above pH 7.0: iron/zinc deficiencies appear.

🪨 Liming Guide (if pH < 5.5)

pH 5.0–5.5 → Apply 1.0–1.5 t/ha agricultural lime
pH 4.5–5.0 → Apply 2.0–2.5 t/ha agricultural lime
pH < 4.5 → Apply 3.0 t/ha lime; re-test after 6 months

📋 Application Method

Broadcast lime evenly; incorporate into top 15 cm; apply 4 weeks before planting.
⚠ Do NOT mix lime with fertiliser — apply at least 2 weeks apart.
